Research

Designing a Better Password Meter

Project: Determime a system to reliably estimate password stength. Then, implement our findings in a password creation flow (e.g. a password meter) in a way that is highly usable and wont annoy users.

Problem: Until recently, there was not a robust way for determining how strong a password is. Existing systems relied on information entropy, which works in theory, but hackers increasingly leverage heuristics to crack passwords, making it more difficult to determine how safe a given password is with only this theory of entropy.

Produced: Data to support our new password strength estimator, results from user studies to ensure our product is highly usable, and a complete frontend

Impact: PNC bank is rolling out our new meter to their customers nationally, and we also provide an open source version of the same system so anyone can take advantage of our research for their own projects.

Can Regular People Take Advantage of Encrypted Messaging

Note that this project is ongoing. We are at early stages of study design.

Project: Determine if the secure smartphone market is actually providing the service they say they are.

Problem: Some secure chat applications are difficult to use for an average person, so the user might entrust sensitive information to a system that is not secure due to not the fault of the security, but the difficulity of ensuring the security is operating as intended.
